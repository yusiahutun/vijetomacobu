Early life

Taylor Fritz was born on October 28 1997. He is the youngest son to Kathy May, a former top 10 tennis player, and Guy Henry Fritz, a former professional tennis player named U.S. Olympic Development Coach of 2016.

The personality grew up with his two siblings, Chris Paben and Kyle Paben, in Rancho Santa Fe in the San Diego metropolitan area. He went to Torrey Pines High School, where he earned the CIF singles title for the San Diego section as a freshman.

A few months into his sophomore year, he decided to do homeschooling to allow him to play full-time in ITF junior events. As a result, in 2015, he was named ITF Junior World Champion. He was the first American to hold that title since Donald Young in 2005.

Career

Taylor began playing professionally in any ITF event when he was 15, where he competed in a low-level Grade 4 tournament in Claremont. In 2015, he reached the quarterfinal of the four junior Grand Slam tournaments, including the French Open final.
