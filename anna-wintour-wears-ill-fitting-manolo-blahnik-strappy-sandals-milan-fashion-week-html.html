Anna Wintour wears ill-fitting Manolo Blahnik strappy sandals at Milan Fashion Week

Time for new shoes Anna? Ms Wintour steps out in ill-fitting heels at Milan Fashion Week - but she's been wearing the same style since the NINETIES

As the editor of American Vogue, Anna Wintour has access to the most stylish footwear around - but it appears she still finds it hard to find shoes that fit.

The 63-year-old was spotted wandering around Milan over the weekend darting between Fashion Week shows, with her pinky toes spilling out of a pair of Manolo Blahnik strappy sandals.

With her big toes awkwardly pointing towards the second digits, Dr. Selene Parekh, an orthopedic surgeon based in North Carolina, told MailOnline that she appears to be developing bunions, often caused by poorly-fitting shoes.

Foot faux pas: Anna Wintour wandered around Milan over the weekend with her toes peeping out of her favorite pair of made-to-measure Manolo Blahnik sandals

Quite a squeeze: Ms Wintour's feet looked far from comfortable, with her big toes awkwardly splayed

'This type of shoe wear, with narrow toe boxes, is known to press the toes against one another and lead to bunions, hammer toes, and claw toes. These deformities can look unsightly,' Dr. Parekh said.

Despite Ms Wintour's feet looking uncomfortably squeezed into the nude heels, they remain her favourite pair.

She has been spotted wearing the same style of slingbacks almost everywhere since 1994, when they were first made-to-measure for her by Manolo Blahnik.

The shoe designer previously told Fashion Front Row that the design was originally inspired by Maria Callas, the great opera singer.

Slipping out: The fashion editor has been wearing the same style of shoe since the mid-Nineties

'Anna asked me to take the ankle wrap from the shoe and add a slingback, as she never likes anything to cut her legs. The shoe then became Callasli,' Mr Blahnik recalled.

To ensure the shoes fit her contours exactly, he made a last - a wooden model of Ms Wintour's foot - around which he sculpts each pair.

They are also produced expressly for her in two skin-coloured tones that perfectly suit her colouring.

Mr Blahnik said that the open toe style, which many women refer to as the 'AW', help showcase Ms Wintour's 'meticulously groomed' feet.

Indeed, on Saturday she sported a clean-looking French manicure on her toes.

Ms Wintour takes delivery of several identical pairs of the shoes, which retail for around $700, each season.

Wardrobe staple: An early sighting of the shoes in June 1999 (left) and at Milan Fashion Week 2003 (right)

Still going strong: At a fundraising event in 2006 (left) and at a film premiere in New York in 2009 (right)

'We’re always discussing materials and adjusting the heel heights and whether or not straps should be wider or stay the same. In the end, it is always up to Anna,' Mr Blahnik added.

Daily Mail columnist, Liz Jones applauded the fashion mogul's simple shoe tastes.

'Like Kate Middleton, who for years has seemed melded to her LK Bennett nude patent courts, Ms Wintour understands that nude goes with everything: jazzy prints, and every colour in the rainbow.

'Her Manolos, like her glossy bob, love of fur and pastel cardis, are a trademark, a constant in an ever-changing sea of trends.'

Mr Blahnik added: 'I am incredibly thankful for Anna’s loyalty and if she chooses to move in another direction, I would also respect that. Betrayed is too strong a word but I’d definitely be hurt.'
