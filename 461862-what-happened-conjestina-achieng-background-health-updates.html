What happened to Conjestina Achieng? Background, health, updates

Conjestina Achieng came into the limelight as one of Kenya's most talented female boxers. She became the first African woman to win an international title, the Vacant WIBF Middleweight. So, what do you know about the former boxer and what happened to her?

Conjestina Achieng is a former Kenyan professional boxer. Her impressive talent saw her post impressive stats and earned her the nickname Hands of Stone. Unfortunately, she has had mental challenges that have seen her in and out of rehabilitation centres.

Conjestina Achieng's biography

Conjestina was born on October 20th, 1977, in Umiru village, Yala division, Siaya District, Kenya. She is the daughter of Clement Adalo and Gertrude Auma. Her father was a retired medical officer. He passed on in 2015.

Achieng was raised alongside nine siblings. She is the fifth born of ten siblings. Her elder brother, Joseph Kusimba, is an ex-boxer who runs a boxing club in Mathare, Nairobi. Not much information about her other siblings is known to the public.

Educational background

The Kenyan boxer was not lucky to have much formal education. She went to St. Jesus Primary School in Yala and discovered her passion for boxing at nine years. However, she did not proceed with her studies, but instead, she ventured into boxing.

Profession

Conjestina Achieng began her career as a boxer in 2000 after graduating through the youth soccer ranks in Nairobi's Makongeni Estate. She was inspired by her elder brother, who was a member of the national boxing team.

Joseph Kusimba nurtured and trained her. He trained her for a month before allowing her to face an opponent in the amateur ranks. After seven amateur bouts, she made her pro debut on June 8th, 2002. She won a four-round decision over Naomi Wanjiku of Kenya.

Achieng earned the nickname Hands of Stone. She became the first African woman to hold an international title. She beat Ugandan Fiona Tugume to take the vacant WIBF Middleweight title.

During her career, the former boxer registered impressive stats. In her career of 27 matches, she won 17 of them, lost 6 and had 4 draws.

Conjestina Achieng's illness

Conjestina became a household name in the mid-2000s. However, the boxer disappeared from the scenes. Many of her fans were left wondering about her whereabouts.

In January 2011, her father appealed to Kenyans to help him take her to the hospital. The boxer's behaviour deteriorated from the norm. She was admitted to the Mathare mental hospital but was later released.

What is Conjestina suffering from? The boxer has mental disabilities. She was diagnosed with Schizoaffective disorder. On September 4th, 2012, a story about her was run on Citizen TV. The story showed her in a state of confusion and utter despair.

Conjestina Achieng's health caught the attention of many viewers. Popular media personalities Carol Radul and Daniel Ndambuki offered their support. Churchill donated Ksh. 180,000 for her house rent. On the other hand, Carol has occasionally visited her home and highlighted her story.

Did Conjestina Achieng burn her house?

In 2021, there were rumours that Conjestina Achieng's house had gone ablaze. It was alleged that the boxer had been involved in torching it down because of frustrations. However, her family disputed the reports.

A family member claimed that the cause of the fire had not been established. All her clothes got burnt, but the house and belts were safe. The fire was inside the house but went off before getting to the roof.

Where is Conjestina Achieng now?

The famous boxer has been in and out of rehabilitation centres. In 2016, she was treated at the Foundation of Hope Rehabilitation Centre in Kisumu. However, she escaped from the facility.

She returned to her rural home in Yala, Siaya County. Her family appealed for help to return her to a rehabilitation facility. Finally, former Nairobi governor Mike Mbuvi Sonko intervened and took her to rehabilitation, and she even showed progress.
