Facts about Khor Virap Monastery

The Khor Virap Monastery, nestled at the foot of Mount Ararat in Armenia, is a site steeped in history, spirituality, and mystery. Let’s uncover 15 enigmatic facts about this ancient place of worship.

The Legendary Foundation

Legend has it that Khor Virap Monastery was founded in the 4th century by Saint Gregory the Illuminator, who played a crucial role in converting Armenia to Christianity. This makes it one of the oldest monasteries in the country.

Accidental Discovery

The breathtaking location of Khor Virap was discovered accidentally in the 17th century. A shepherd, seeking refuge from a storm, found a hidden entrance to a underground chamber, which turned out to be the main sanctuary of the monastery.

Deep Underground Dungeons

Khor Virap is famous for its deep underground dungeons, where Saint Gregory was imprisoned for 13 years by the then-Pagan King Tiridates III. These dark and eerie dungeons add to the mysterious aura surrounding the monastery.

Stunning Views of Mount Ararat

One of the most captivating features of Khor Virap is the uninterrupted view it offers of the majestic Mount Ararat. This snow-capped volcano, often associated with the biblical story of Noah’s Ark, adds to the spiritual energy of the site.

Pilgrimage Destination

Khor Virap is a popular pilgrimage destination, attracting believers from all around the world. Pilgrims come here seeking spiritual solace, making their way through the complex labyrinth of chambers and courtyards.

The Underground Holy Pit

Within the monastery, there is a sacred pit known as the “holy pit,” where Saint Gregory was imprisoned. It is said that through his unwavering faith, he survived the confinement and emerged unscathed, ultimately bringing about the conversion of Armenia.

Mysterious Healing Powers

Legend has it that the holy pit of Khor Virap possesses healing properties. It is believed that by crawling into the pit and touching the sacred stones, one can seek spiritual and physical healing.

Connection to Ancient Myths

Khor Virap Monastery holds a significant place in Armenian mythology. It is believed that this sacred site sits on the same spot where the mythical god Ara the Beautiful was buried, adding an air of mystique to the monastery.

The Miraculous Eternity Flame

The Eternity Flame, which burns continuously in the monastery grounds, is believed to have been ignited by Saint Gregory himself. The flame represents the eternal connection between heaven and earth and is considered a symbol of divine presence.

Fascinating Frescoes

The monastery’s interior is adorned with intricate frescoes dating back centuries. These artworks depict scenes from biblical stories, saints, and martyrs, providing a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Armenia.

A Site for Baptism

Many Christian pilgrims choose Khor Virap as the site for their baptism. The sacred water of the well within the monastery is believed to possess cleansing and purifying properties, making it an ideal location for this important sacrament.

An Architectural Marvel

Khor Virap showcases exceptional architectural craftsmanship. The monastery’s design amalgamates different styles, including Armenian, Byzantine, and Gothic influences, making it a visual spectacle.

Astronomical Significance

Khor Virap’s location is said to have astronomical significance. According to ancient beliefs, the alignment of the monastery with specific celestial bodies can have a profound impact on spiritual energies, making it an important site for astrological studies.

A Symbol of Armenian Resilience

Khor Virap Monastery stands as a symbol of Armenian resilience through the ages. Despite wars, invasions, and earthquakes, the monastery has withstood the test of time, serving as a testament to the unwavering faith and determination of the Armenian people.

A: Yes, Khor Virap Monastery is open to visitors. It welcomes both tourists and pilgrims who wish to explore the site and learn about its history.

Q: Can visitors climb Mount Ararat from Khor Virap Monastery?

A: No, climbing Mount Ararat from Khor Virap Monastery is not permitted. However, the monastery offers stunning views of the majestic mountain from its grounds.
