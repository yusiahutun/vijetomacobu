Mark Selby, Barry Hawkins and snooker referee left in tears of laughter as cue breaks

Barry Hawkins and Mark Selby both suffered a fit of the giggles during the first frame of their Championship League clash at the Morningside Arena in Leicester yesterday.

Hawkins, trailing by 54 points, left Selby snookered behind the green with two reds down at the opposite end of the table.

With almost the entire length of the table to cue over to reach the white, Selby asked the referee to supply him with the extended rest.

As the match official prepared to place the device on the table, the first third broke apart from the rest and landed with a thud on the baize.

The unexpected mishap caused both players to laugh out loud and then continue to snigger uncontrollably.

For the next five minutes both players struggled to regain their composure as Hawkins bravely battled on in an attempt to win the frame.

After fouling on the yellow he conceded the frame to Selby - but the Kent-born player went on the claim the win by three frames to one.

Meanwhile, Neil Robertson has been on the receiving end of plenty of banter after his 'SatNav' blunder last week.

The Australian was scheduled to face Ian Burns in the World Open qualifiers at the Barnsley Metrodome in South Yorkshire on Friday.
