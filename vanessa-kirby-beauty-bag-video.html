Inside my beauty bag video

English actress Vanessa Kirby has a beauty philosophy similar to her fashion formula, that of knowing what suits her and keeping it simple.

"My job has definitely shaped my approach to beauty; I don’t think I had one before," she tells us in this exclusive video (above), where she reveals her most cherished products. "You end up learning quite a lot about things that work for you and things that don’t," she says, adding that the hair and make-up pros she gets to work with on and off-set "have reeducated" her, aesthetically speaking.

Josh Shinner

She recalls making “several catastrophic mistakes” with beauty when she was a teenager. They include wearing “way too much foundation” that was “pointlessly caked”, “dodgy eyeliner”, “the brightest neon blue eyeshadow” and “bad haircuts” – photos of which she can't bring herself to look at now. “Everyone wanted the Jennifer Aniston haircut didn’t they? I had that a few times. Disaster!”

Now, the BAFTA TV Award-winning actress oozes elegance with a minimal approach. Her make-up base comprises the sheer Sisley Phyto Tint Nude in ‘1C Petal’ for foundation, her "lifesaver", the Nars Radiant Creamy Concealer in ‘Vanilla’, and the Armani Beauty Neo Nude A-Contour – an easily blendable contouring pigment. "I’m really obsessed with it," she shares, "I use it as everything" – on her eyes and lips as well as along her cheekbones. For eyeshadow she also loves the "subtle" Bobbi Brown Long-Wear Cream Shadow Stick in ‘Golden Bronze’ and the "really thin" Kimiko Fine Eyebrow Pencil Automatique for enhancing her already epic eyebrows.

When it comes to skincare, Kirby admits she loves SkinCeuticals, calling out the brand's Phyto Corrective Essence Mist as an unsung hero. "I feel like it actually does something," as do the 111Skin eye patches and face masks – the best thing she's discovered on sets, she says, from that of The Crown to Mission: Impossible – Fallout. "They actually work... especially if you haven’t had much sleep."

Her career has also contributed to the health of her hair, though in a less positive way. Needing to change colours to play different characters, "my hair has been fried from so much peroxide," she admits, discovering that the Virtue Polish Un-Frizz Cream works wonders on undoing some of the damage. "It tries its best against my frizz," she reviews.

It's her relationship with fragrance though that Kirby talks about on an especially emotional level. Showing her Cartier La Panthère Parfum, of which she is an ambassador, she explains that she not only adores it for its scent, but for its powerful association of a panther – a different kind of feminine symbol to something typically "girlie or flowery".

Cartier Le Panthère Parfum

Cartier Le Panthère Parfum

"I guess by association, when you wear something, it makes you feel a certain way. It definitely makes me feel stronger; you think, 'Oh, if I didn’t have to apologise for who I am, if I didn’t have to edit or compromise...' I think it helps me identify which realm I’m going into, or which zone." She continues, adding that for "women generally it feels like now’s the time to claim more space or have more permission to be the biggest we can be, and that takes loads of work; it’s a daily thing." For Kirby her fragrance ritual is regular reminder.

Of course, she doesn't apply it with abandon, but prefers to wear it for herself, and not others. When it comes to perfume, "nothing too overpowering is the key," she feels. "I think we all know that from being in lifts with people wearing too much," which – the star admits – she probably did in the past.

We'll take Kirby as proof that ascribing to the less-is-more maxim pays off.
