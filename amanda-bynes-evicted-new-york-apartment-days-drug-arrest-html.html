It seemed things could not get worse for Amanda Bynes.

But 10 days after her drug arrest, it has emerged the former Nickelodeon favourite has been evicted from her New York City apartment, where police say they saw her throw a marijuana bong outside her 36th-floor window. 

The retired 27-year-old actress was kicked out of the the midtown Manhattan high rise on Tuesday, according to InTouch.

According to the insider, she 'was notified that she is no longer welcome as a tenant in the building in light of recent events.'

The report follows little over a week after Amanda's May 23 arrest following complaints from neighbours about her alleged drug use.

The star was booked for criminal possession of marijuana, reckless endangerment and felony tampering with physical evidence.

Tall tale: She is facing jail after allegedly throwing a bong from her former flat on the 36th floor

Amanda has denied any wrongdoing and initially vowed to sue police for sexual harassment.

The former actress claimed an NYPD police officer had sexually harassed her in a rambling tweet, that he had 'slapped her vagina.'

Police have dismissed Amanda's claims that she was 'touched inappropriately' during her arrest on drug charges last week.

'Internal Affairs investigators have found no evidence to corroborate Ms. Bynes' allegations,' the NYPD said in a statement.

'To the contrary, a credible civilian witness who was with the officers throughout told investigators that none touched Ms. Bynes inappropriately or otherwise engaged in misconduct at any time.'

Amanda's behaviour has become increasingly erratic since she left California to pursue a fashion career, posting pictures of herself to Twitter, attacking celebrities in a variety of posts, claiming she needs another nose job and wearing a number of different colored wigs.

Troubled: The former child star wearing her trademark wig after being arrested on May 23

And she told TMZ on Sunday she had actually gone through with her plan to have her nose fixed, saying: 'I have no bandage on. It's healing on it's own like my doctor asked.

'I'm getting one more in 3 weeks, they are short amazing surgeries done while I'm awake but under general anesthesia. It's almost perfect.'

She also took to Twitter to say: 'My dad is as ugly as RuPaul! So thankful I look nothing like you both! I had nose surgery after my mug shots so my nose and I are gorgeous!'

The drag queen was not too amused, and fired back with, 'Derogatory slurs are ALWAYS an outward projection of a person's own poisonous self-loathing @AmandaBynes.'

Her attorney, Richard A Hutton, last week said the former actress seemed 'fine' in every interaction he has ever had with her, even in recent phone conversations since her arrest.

Hutton, who is representing the 27-year-old teen star for a string of motor vehicle incidents since April 2012 of last year, told People magazine he believes the whole case has been 'blown out of proportion.'

Hutton went on to explain: 'The New York case doesn't seem like much of a case, really. I mean, come on.

'Even if you believe everything you hear, it's something that amounts to virtually nothing.

'And it's my understanding there's not much evidence to back that case up.

'There's no physical evidence to back it up. I understand there's no marijuana, no pipe.'

And of his client he said: 'I think Amanda's fine. I have no problems with her whatsoever.

'She's a fine client. Every time I've talked to her, I've had nice, attorney-client normal relations with her.'

Hutton is not Bynes's attorney in the state of New York and is only representing her for a number of different charges she has accumulated since last year.

The next hearing for her DUI case is set for July 18.
