Foundations are the most integral layer of cosmetics for every makeup lover. For a dewy, matte, or glossy look, the first step is to invest in one of the best MAC foundations, the OG of makeup products. A good foundation will set the overall tone of the makeup but if your foundation does not match your skin tone or oxidizes too soon, then you’re giving fashion disaster a free pass into your life. Austrian beauty blogger Lizzie says, “Foundations come in different forms, there’s BB cream, powder foundation, tinted, foundation stick, and a bunch more but the most common type of foundation is the liquid one. Depending on your skin type, you can find different formulations.”

The purpose of the foundation is to provide your skin with a flawless appearance, the degree of which can vary from person to person. While some people may like to wear their foundation to mimic a no-makeup look, others might want to go all out and create a makeup look that enhances their existing complexion, giving them medium to full coverage. Whatever the purpose is, MAC cosmetics has a foundation for every skin and every need. Here’s a list of the best MAC foundations, so you can decide your shade of beauty.

    How Long Does a Mac Foundation Last?

    Although it might seem like the toughest task in the world to throw away your favorite lipstick or foundation, it's crucial to follow makeup expiration dates.“The ingredients and formula are going to start to change and if you apply these products to your skin they could cause chemical burns, breakouts, skin irritation, and issues that's why it is essential to pay attention to how long you've had a product," Canada-based Skincare expert, Ashley says. 

    But here's the tricky part--The use-by date on your makeup products, however, is dependent on the first day you use them rather than the date of purchase, unlike the contents of your refrigerator. If you ever find yourself in doubt, all products have a tiny symbol that resembles a jar with a number or letter on the inside that denotes the PAO, or period after opening. The product's shelf life after opening is indicated by the numbers that come before the letter M. Often these foundations typically have a shelf life of six months to a year. Even though the foundation can last for a few years if it isn't opened, it is best to replace it after six to twelve months if the seal has been broken.

    "Especially if you have sensitive skin, you should keep a close eye on your products. In case the expiry date is not available, every beauty product will have a batch code number which you can use to call the company and they will be able to tell you when that product was manufactured, or you can use the website called Cosmetic Calculator which will do the work for you," adds Ashley. 

    Another tell-tale sign of expired formula is a foul smell or change in its appearance. Separation is yet another indication that liquid or creamy foundation is long past its prime. Therefore, the next time you notice an immiscible layer forming in your foundation, don't shake the bottle for reuse; simply discard it. You can also prolong the shelf life of your foundation by ensuring minimal contact of your fingers from the neck of the bottle or tube.  Also, keep foundations away from high-temperature and damp areas (like your bathroom), as these conditions encourage the growth of bacteria and hasten the spoiling process.
