Carrie Coon’s method of career advancement Career Carrie started her acting career in theaters, where she performed on stage. Coon started working at the American Players Theatre after graduating, where she remained for four seasons.

She performed in three plays that year, including “Romeo and Juliet,” “The Matchmaker,” and “Measure for Measure.” She penned “The Belle’s Stratagem” for them in 2008. Similarly to that, she then joined the Goodman Theatre in Chicago, where “Magnolia” was her debut production there in 2009.

After that, she started acting in plays in several theaters, including the 2010’s production of “Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?” at the Steppenwolf Theatre Company. Her rise to prominence was sparked by this stage play.

In terms of her filmography, Carrie debuted in 2012 short “One in a Million.” She then played Margo Dunne in the film “Gone Girl,” which also starred Ben Affleck, Rosamund Pike, Neil Patrick Harris, Tyler Perry, and Kim Dickens.
