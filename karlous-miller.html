Career and Professional Life

Moving on to his career, he started his comedy career with Quincy Bond’s Phat Comedy group in 2008. Moreover, he made his television debut on Robert Townsend’s Partners in Crime – The New Generation. Following that he has appeared on various shows like “The Mo’Nique Show,” “Black Card Revoked,” “Hell Date,” and MTV’s “Yo Momma”.

Furthermore, he also reached the semifinals of Bill Bellamy’s show “Who’re Got Jokes.”Also, in 2015,  he launched his podcast The 85 South Show with his friends DC Young Fly, and Chico where they discuss various topics, interact with the audience, and freestyle on songs.

Besides that, he has performed all over the world, including in St. Thomas, Germany, London, and Kuwait. His biggest comedy influences are Redd Foxx, Bernie Mac, Richard Pryor, Mo’Nique, Eddie Murphy, Cedric the Entertainer, and Steve Harvey.

In addition, he also enjoys music and has collaborated with various artists such as Jermaine Dupri, Gucci Mane, Dem Franchize Boyz, Case, Grandaddy Souf, Big Oomp, and Grand Hustle Comedy.

Moreover, in 2018, he confirmed on the Breakfast Club that he would not appear in the upcoming season of Wild N Out and that he had been fired. But later, he was eventually brought back to the show due to fan requests.
