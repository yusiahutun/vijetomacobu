Fans delighted as Helen Chamberlain returns to TV five years after Soccer AM exit

Soccer AM fans were delighted to see Helen Chamberlain back on TV on Thursday evening, five years after her 22-year stint on the Sky Sports show came to an end.

Chamberlain was back on screens doing interviews for the inaugural World Seniors Darts Championship, which got underway at the Circus Tavern in Purfleet.

Chamberlain enjoyed banter with the likes of Bob Anderson, Robert Thornton, Peter Manley and Deta Hedman before their respective encounters, with Ray Stubbs fronting the show.

Twitter users were pleased to see her back in action with BT Sport, with the tournament taking place across the weekend with the final on Sunday - which could include darts icon Phil Taylor.

Chamberlain started on the Saturday morning show back in 1995 after a stint on Nickelodeon, and also presented shows on Channel 4 and for Sky Poker.

The Torquay United fan was then replaced on the show as they brought in Lloyd Griffith and Jimmy Bullard alongside John Fendley.

The show said after her exit: "Farewell and thank you to Mrs Soccer AM. This place won't be the same without you."

Chamberlain said in an interview with The Athletic in 2020: "I left in 2017. I spent nearly half of my life sitting on that sofa. I was there for nearly 23 years.

"That’s a long time to have any job never mind a job in broadcasting. I had the farm to see to. I wanted to rescue more dogs and breed sheep and chickens.

"So I didn’t have time for Saturday mornings any more."
