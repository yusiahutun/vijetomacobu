History and Foundation

Sc Corinthians Paulista, commonly known as Corinthians, is a Brazilian football club based in São Paulo. Established on September 1, 1910, the club has a rich history spanning over a century.

Nickname

Corinthians is commonly referred to as Timão, which means “big team” in Portuguese. This nickname reflects the club’s passionate fan base and strong legacy in Brazilian football.

Stadium

The team’s home ground is the iconic Arena Corinthians. Located in the São Paulo neighborhood of Itaquera, the stadium has a seating capacity of over 49,000 spectators.

Corinthians has enjoyed great success in regional tournaments, particularly the Campeonato Paulista. The club has won the competition a record 30 times, solidifying its dominance in São Paulo football.

Sc Corinthians Paulista has an impressive record in national and international competitions. The club has won the Campeonato Brasileiro Serie A seven times and claimed the prestigious Copa Libertadores and FIFA Club World Cup in 2012.
