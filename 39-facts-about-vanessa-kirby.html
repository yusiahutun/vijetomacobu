Vanessa Kirby was born on April 18, 1988, in London, England.

Originally from Wimbledon, Kirby began her journey towards stardom in the renowned British capital.

She attended Lady Eleanor Holles School and started acting at a young age.

Kirby discovered her passion for performing arts during her time at Lady Eleanor Holles School, where she began honing her acting skills.

Kirby graduated from the prestigious University of Exeter with a degree in English.

She pursued higher education despite her growing interest in acting and holds a BA in English literature from the University of Exeter.

Her breakthrough role came in 2010 in the BBC series “The Hour”.

Kirby’s talent shone through in her portrayal of Marnie Madden, earning her critical acclaim and recognition within the entertainment industry.

Vanessa Kirby gained international fame for her role as Princess Margaret in the Netflix series “The Crown”.

Her captivating performance as the effervescent and complex Princess Margaret garnered praise from audiences and critics worldwide.

She won a BAFTA Award for Best Supporting Actress for her role in “The Crown”.

Kirby’s exceptional acting skills were acknowledged by the British Academy of Film and Television Arts (BAFTA) with this prestigious award.

Vanessa Kirby made her big-screen debut in the 2011 film “Love/Loss”.

She showcased her versatility as an actress in this emotional drama, setting the stage for her successful transition to Hollywood.
