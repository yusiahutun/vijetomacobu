Curvy model defies trolls who say she 'eats too much' with sexy lingerie shoot

A plus size model refuses to let cruel comments hold her back.

Emily Jones says trolls often assume she's "lazy" and "eats too much" – just because she has curves.

She believes this is a result of natural bodies not being commonplace in mainstream media.

The 31-year-old told Daily Star: “I feel there is a misconception that we are just the sideline chubby funny friend in the RomCom, providing some comic relief, never the sexy, complex and vibrant ‘main character’.

“I think people can be really judgmental of larger bodied women. One of my pet peeves is when people believe we are just lazy and eat too much – bodies come in all shapes, sizes and abilities, it’s a bit more nuanced than just ‘eating less and moving more’.

“The truth is larger women are out there living their best lives and we need to see that represented in our media!”

Emily campaigns for body positivity and has achieved a huge following of over 230,000 Instagram fans who love her sizzling photos.

The brunette beauty loves to flaunt her body in lingerie and swimwear on social media to normalise seeing plus sized bodies and encourage self-love.

But, when it comes to feeling sexy herself how does she boost her confidence?

Emily, who splits her time between London and Malta said: “think once I decided to make a rule to always stay true to myself, I felt a surge in self-confidence.

“Creating content for my online pages has also made me more confident, knowing my angles and putting my best foot forward.”

She added: “I love to treat myself to a little moment of luxury, and give myself some TLC!

“This can mean different things on different days but I enjoy simple things like a hot shower followed by my skincare routine and slathering myself head to toe in an aromatherapy oil.”

And, the curvy bombshell’s favourite thing to wear is cheeky lingerie.

Emily noted: “I love modelling underwear, I think it’s such a crucial part of feeling confident, and having a great-fitting and comfy base to your outfit is where the magic starts!

“It can really help you to feel good, even if it is only you who knows you have it on.”

But, the model thinks there’s a long way to go before fuller figured women are fully celebrated thanks to extreme beauty standards and a lack of representation.

Emily said: “We are expected to look like the perfectly airbrushed and curated images we see online - but the reality is that those images are far from how the subject actually appears in real life.

“It’s an impossible standard that even our most perfect-seeming online icons cannot meet.

“I feel this impacts women’s mental health and self-confidence, we are constantly comparing ourselves to a fictional existence.

“That is part of why I’m so keen to promote feeling your best whatever stage of your physical journey you are at, with my untouched photos and videos.

“Some representation of different bodies and abilities, some diversity, is refreshing in our world of picture-perfect images."

Emily, who posts beautiful snaps of herself on Instagram page @miss___jones, added that people often don’t think about plus sized women in a positive way.

She also campaigns for invisible disability awareness after being diagnosed with inflammatory arthritis, lipidema and fibromyalgia.
