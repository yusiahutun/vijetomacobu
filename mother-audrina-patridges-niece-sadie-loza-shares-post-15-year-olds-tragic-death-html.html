Mother of Audrina Patridge's niece Sadie Loza shares post after the 15-year-old's tragic death

The mother of Audrina Patridge's 15 year-old niece has taken to social media to memorialize her daughter after the teen's sudden death on Tuesday - shortly after The Hills alum shared her own emotional tribute to the youngster.  

Casey Loza, the mom of the late Sadie Loza, shared the tearful message to Instagram, days after sharing video that showed her daughter, surrounded by family, happy and blowing out candles for a 15th birthday celebration.

The post was originally shared last year, but Casey evidently considered it an apt memorial to her late daughter, and uploaded it to her stories again on Wednesday. 

Casey - who is also Audrey's sister - had confirmed in an earlier post that Sadie had died on Valentine's Day, but did not reveal the cause of the teen's death.

The mourning mother proceeded to share another to further memorialize her child, with a heartfelt a message centered around God and a photo of a young Sadie wearing a cat Snapchat filter.

Tagging her daughter in the post, Casey wrote: 'God gave me this face to get people’s attention and this brain to change their perspectives.'

Sadie is Casey’s eldest daughter from her previous relationship with Aaron Hershman, who was shot dead on the streets Chino, California, in February 2012 at age 29, shortly after splitting with Casey and the birth of their daughter.

'Sadie Raine Loza has left this Earth for the heavens,' wrote Casey, who has two other kids, aged 12 and 3, with husband Kyle.

'Writing this was the hardest thing I ever have had to do, not knowing the right words.'

Casey went on to offer followers an anecdote she said Sadie had imparted on her herself before her sudden death. 

I'’ll leave you with this,' she wrote. 'Sadie liked to remind me that the first law of thermodynamics aka (Law of Conservation of Energy) works in all spheres of life.

'Energy cannot be created or destroyed, it can only be changed from one form to another.'

She continued: 'Transformation is a better word than death. Her story will save countless lives, her memory will never be forgotten. Rest In Peace you brilliant starseed. You’re with your creator. We will miss you dearly. I love you.'
