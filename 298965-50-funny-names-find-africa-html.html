50+ funny names you can only find in Africa

In Africa, passing down funny names from one generation to another might be deliberate, but the intentions are free of malice and any sort of prejudice, other than strong cultural beliefs. To put things into perspective, no African knows his or her name is funny, especially their traditional names, until s/he gets a second opinion about the name from a friend or close associate. If anything, Africans understand their names as a family’s emblem and a title worn with pride, but not a reason to laugh; which makes things even more interesting.

Most Africans name their children based on the prevailing conditions at the time of birth. It is also common for them to name children according to what they wish for the child. This is especially so for Africans living in Namibia, Zimbabwe and South Africa. However, with the introduction of English during the colonial time came a rather funny shift in the naming process. Initially, Africans had traditional names both the first name -according to the situation at birth or wishes of the parents towards the child, and their sir name, - which was mostly the family name.

Funny Kenyan names

By now you must be wondering what are good African names? If the names listed above are not good, then you need to manage your expectation because Kenyan funny names are just that; funny!

1. Apoko Nyang’au

This is a popular Kisii name, which probably means well. However, for avid Swahili speakers, this name means a dirty dog or bloody brute. With people being lazy judges, this name is judged at face value, which often leaves people in laughter.

2. Maskini

For a Kenyan, a Swahili speaker for that matter, giving such a name to your child is quite surprising. However, to the bearers of this name, Maskini is a symbol of pride worn with admiration.

4. Wamalwa

This is a popular Luhya boy name. Arguably, it is by far a hilarious name for a Luhya. It means that the boy child has been born during the brewing season.

5. Mugeci

Mugeci is a lady who loves gossip. She comes as half Luhya and half Kikuyu. It is believed that only a Luhya or a Chef can wife her for reasons beyond my scope of wisdom.

6. Nyambura

According to the Kikuyu tribe, Nyambura means born during the rain. To the Luo, Nyambura means a kitten. It is a word coined from Mbura, which means cat.
