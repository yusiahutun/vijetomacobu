DaBaby net worth 2020: record deal, sales, and charges per show

DaBaby, born Jonathan Lyndale Kirk, is an award-winning singer, rapper, songwriter, and entrepreneur. Although he has been in the music scene for less than ten years, the artist has crafted his sound and, more importantly, produced mega hits. As a result, he has sold thousands of records and grown his fan base. In the past six years, DaBaby net worth has also grown significantly.

How did DaBaby get famous? Well, like most rappers, he started releasing mixtapes in December of 2014. Although the debut mixtape — Non-Fiction — did not create enough buzz in the Hip-hop scene, it was an essential foundation for the young rapper. Since then, he has recorded and released four more mixtapes and three albums.

How much is DaBaby worth? According to the Celebrity Net Worth, the rapper has a net worth of $3 million. However, the Charlotte rapper does not share pictures of his cars and houses on his social media pages. Therefore, it is hard to quantify the worth of his cars and homes.

Record and distribution deals

Who is Da Baby signed to? The award-winning artist is in a joint agreement with two record labels — Interscope Records and South Coast Music Records. Interscope Records first gave him a record deal in 2019. A few months later, South Coast Music Group came on board as a joint partner to Interscope Records.

The two record labels are heavily involved in the rapper’s music and life as an artist. As a 360 deal, they market his music, fund production, coordinate distribution contracts, and licencing.

YouTube revenue

While YouTube has one of the lowest revenue per view, DaBaby has impressive numbers. The rapper joined the platform in 2015. In the last five years, he has accumulated more than 2.3 billion views and six million subscribers.

Unlike other platforms, YouTube has different approaches in calculating revenue. Fortunately, DaBaby is under joint management which has maximized his income from this platform.

All DaBaby’s songs on YouTube have UGC, “user-generated content” tag. If any content creator uses any of his songs, the rapper gets a cut. Due to his popularity, most content creators use his songs in video introductions or background music.

Also, most of DaBaby’s songs on YouTube have a VEVO tag. As a result, his songs have a higher Ad rate from Google, and so are his revenues per view are higher.

Based on these factors and consistent growth of his channel, DaBaby earns at least $4,644,000 per year from the platform. Since he is in a 360 deal with Interscope, management takes the largest cut. It is also worth noting that his income is subject to tax cuts.
