The Largest Mosque in Southeast Asia

Istiqlal Mosque, located in Jakarta, Indonesia, holds the prestigious title of being the largest mosque in Southeast Asia.

A Symbol of Independence

Istiqlal Mosque is a symbol of Indonesia’s fight for independence. The word “istiqlal” itself means independence in Arabic.

Designed by a Christian Architect

While Istiqlal Mosque is a prominent Islamic landmark, it was designed by a Protestant Christian architect named Frederich Silaban.

A Fusion of Architectural Styles

The mosque’s design is a harmonious blend of modern and traditional architectural styles, incorporating elements of Indonesian, Arabic, and Indian influences.

The Magnificent Dome

Istiqlal Mosque features a massive central dome, adorned with floral motifs and calligraphy, representing the beauty and significance of Islamic art.

The Grand Prayer Hall

Inside the mosque, you will find a spacious prayer hall capable of accommodating over 120,000 worshipers during special occasions and congregational prayers.

The Exquisite Mihrab

The mihrab, a niche in the wall that indicates the direction of Mecca, is intricately decorated with mosaic tiles and exquisite carvings.

A Stunning Minaret

Istiqlal Mosque boasts a towering minaret measuring 66 meters in height, providing a breathtaking view of Jakarta’s skyline.

An Impressive Quran Collection

The mosque houses an extensive collection of Quranic manuscripts, rare Islamic books, and artifacts that showcase the rich Islamic heritage.

A Welcoming Environment

Istiqlal Mosque not only serves as a place of worship but also as a center for interfaith dialogue, fostering unity and understanding among different religious communities.

A Hub for Islamic Activities

Various Islamic events and activities, including Quran recitation competitions, religious seminars, and educational programs, are regularly held at Istiqlal Mosque.

A Tourist Attraction

Istiqlal Mosque attracts a significant number of tourists from around the world who come to marvel at its architectural beauty and learn about its historical significance.

An Iconic Landmark for Jakarta

Istiqlal Mosque has become an iconic symbol of Jakarta, representing the city’s multicultural identity and religious tolerance.

A Place of Peace and Tranquility

Visitors to Istiqlal Mosque often describe feeling a sense of serenity and tranquility within its peaceful surroundings.

A Testament to Indonesian Unity

Istiqlal Mosque stands as a testament to the spirit of unity and diversity that characterizes the Indonesian nation.

A Destination for Eid Celebrations

During the Islamic holiday of Eid al-Fitr, Istiqlal Mosque becomes a focal point for celebrations, attracting thousands of Muslims who come to offer prayers and seek blessings.

A Monument of Faith

Istiqlal Mosque serves as a constant reminder of the power of faith and the importance of religious freedom in Indonesia.

FAQs

1. When was the Istiqlal Mosque built?
The construction of the Istiqlal Mosque began in 1961 and was completed in 1978.

2. How big is the Istiqlal Mosque?
The mosque covers an area of around 12.7 hectares (31.4 acres) and can accommodate up to 120,000 worshippers.

3. Is the Istiqlal Mosque open to non-Muslim visitors?
Yes, the Istiqlal Mosque welcomes visitors of all faiths who want to admire its magnificent architecture and learn about its cultural significance.

4. Can I take photos inside the mosque?
Yes, photography is allowed inside the mosque, but it is advised to be respectful and mindful of those who are praying.

5. Are there any dress code requirements to visit the mosque?
Visitors are expected to dress modestly, with shoulders and knees covered. Scarves are also provided at the entrance for women who need to cover their hair.
