Finn Wittrock is a talented American actor and screenwriter. Early Life and Childhood

Finn Wittrock was born on October 28, 1984, and he is currently 37 years old. Finn Wittrock hails from Lenox, Massachusetts, the U.S., and his zodiac sign is Scorpio. Talking about his family, Finn’s parents are his mother Kate Claire Crowley, and his father Peter L. Wittrock, Sr.

Finn’s mother is a professor of occupational therapy at the University of Southern California whereas his father is an actor. Likewise, Finn has a younger brother named Dylan.

Finn grew up at the Shakespeare & Company theatre as a child. This is where Finn’s father worked. At his father’s workplace, Finn often acted as a pageboy or messenger.

Likewise, as a teenager, Finn attended the Los Angeles County High School for the Arts. There, Finn would act, make films, and be a part of movement classes for actors.

After his graduation, Finn was accepted into The Juilliard School. However, he turned them down in the hopes of working in Los Angeles.

Subsequently, Finn acted in some episodes of TV shows. However, he was often rejected. Following this, Finn applied for Juilliard again and was accepted.

This time, Finn attended the popular acting school. At Juilliard, Finn was a member of the drama division’s Group 37 from 2004 to 2008.

Moreover, Finn was also an active participant in the drama department. He also starred in many theatrical productions. Likewise, Finn won the Juilliard Journal Award. He was recognized for his outstanding contributions to Juilliard’s newspaper, The Juilliard Journal.

Further, Finn received the drama division’s Stephanie Palmer McClelland Scholarship. From Juilliard, Finn graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in the year 2008.

Career and Professional Life

Caption: Finn on the poster of ‘Locating Silver Lake’. Source: Instagram

Professionally, Finn Wittrock is a talented actor as well as a screenwriter. The actor began his career with several guest roles on TV shows. Finn then debuted with the film Halloweentown High in 2004. The actor then returned to films with the 2010 film Twelve.

Likewise, Finn was a regular in All My Children from 2009 to 2011. In the year 2011, Finn performed in The Illusion and also had his Broadway debut with Happy Loman.

The former is the playwright Tony Kushner’s Off-Broadway play whereas the latter is the 2012 revival of Arthur Miller’s play Death of a Salesman. Likewise, the director for Happy Loman was Mike Nichols.

Then in the year 2014, Finn gained more fame for his appearances in films including The Normal Heart, Noah, and Unbroken. The famous actor also gained a Primetime Emmy Award nomination for playing the role of Dandy Mott in American Horror Story: Freak Show.
