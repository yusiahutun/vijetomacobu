Fossil of 'mythical Chinese Dragon' uncovered as experts find new remains

published on May 21, 2024
The remains of a 'mythical Chinese Dragon' have been discovered for the first time. Palaeontologists have now pieced together the fossil of the 240-million-year-old marine reptile, known as Dinocephalosaurus orientalis. The remnants were discovered in Guizhou Province, southern China. The preserved remains come from the Triassic period of China and measure a whopping five metres (16ft).
