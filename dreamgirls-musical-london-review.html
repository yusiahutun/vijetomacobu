Dreamgirls musical London review

Dreamgirls

It's taken 25 years, but the Dreamgirls musical has finally made the transition from Broadway to the West End. If you make one excursion from your sofa this Christmas break, make it a trip to The Savoy Theatre to see the story of three young women with huge voices heading for stardom. Allow us to convince you.

1. It's empowering

You'll leave the theatre feeling as if you could fly. The moral of the story is don't be afraid to dream and believe in yourself. The three leads are essentially career women, two of whom are momentarily derailed by a charismatic man with questionable motives. Needless to say, girl power and talent wins out. The plot is loosely based on the rise and group dynamic of Diana Ross and The Supremes.

2. The mind-blowing vocals

Glee's Amber Riley takes on the role of the diva Effie White, with a set of lungs to impress even the most hardened soul. All three of the leads are hugely talented, but Riley steals the show with lashings of charisma and talent. Her emotional, powerful track, 'I'm Not Going', will leave you tearful and covered in goosebumps, while her version of 'I Am Changing' will make you want to stand on your chair and whoop. Expect standing ovations throughout the show, not just at the end.

3. It's better than the film

This is not an admission anyone would make lightly; we're talking about Beyoncé and Jennifer Hudson here. However, witnessing such strong vocals live and in such close proximity is, unsurprisingly, more powerful than watching them on screen ever could be.

4. The Gucci-esque suits

The bejewelled gowns of the fictional girl group Dreams are as glamorous as you'd expect from a trip of fledgling divas, but it's the embroidered, flared suits of fellow performer Jimmy Early (a character inspired by James Brown) that really caught our eye. Jimi Hendrix-meets-Gucci, this is tailoring to truly dance in – as proven in the show.

5. The glitter and crystals

Swarovski collaborated with the show to contribute one million glittering crystals, resulting in the most dazzling set imaginable. Everything sparkles – the backdrop, the costumes, the curtains and the stage. Even the heels of the actresses' shoes are adorned in beautiful jewels.

6. It's uplifting

There are few musicals that imbue such energy, optimism and passion. And what could be a better New Year-watch than a tale about ambition, self-fulfilment and heart?
