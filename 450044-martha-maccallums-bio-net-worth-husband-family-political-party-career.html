Early life

The journalist was born Martha Bowes MacCallum on 31 January 1964 (age 58) in Wyckoff, New Jersey, United States. She is the daughter of Elizabeth B. MacCallum (mother) and Douglas C. MacCallum Jr. (father).

MacCallum’s parents worked as teachers. While her father had a degree in teaching, her mother had a degree and a master's in education. Martha grew up in Wyckoff, New Jersey, alongside her two sisters, Lisa Ann MacCallum and Jane Elizabeth MacCallum.

She attended Ramapo High School in Franklin Lakes, New Jersey, and graduated in 1982. After her high school graduation, MacCallum joined St. Lawrence University and received a Degree in Government in 1986.

College career

Martha MacCallum’s first job was at a village cheese shop in Wyckoff, New Jersey. Then, after graduating from St Lawrence University in 1986, she studied for two more years at New York’s Circle in the Square Theatre School.

She helped found the Miranda Theatre Company, a non-profit organisation dedicated to developing and producing new plays. While studying at Square Theatre School, Martha worked as a waitress.

Martha’s early aspirations were to be a film director and actress, and she played the Viola in the Twelfth Night. She gave up her aspirations and found her way into journalism. She worked as a fact-checker at a financial magazine. In 1991, she got her first job in TV as a production assistant at Wall Street Journal Television.

Professional career

Between 1991 and 1996, she served as an anchor and business news correspondent for The Wall Street Journal Report, Business USA, and World Market Outlook. In 1996, she left the company and joined WBIS-TV as an anchor and reporter.

The stint at WBIS-TV was short. Martha then joined CNBC in 1997 as an anchor. She also worked for NBC and anchored many programmes, including Morning Call with Martha MacCallum and Ted David and Checkpoint.

In 2004, Martha joined Fox News Channel. She has been the lead reporter on election exit polls in America since 2004. Furthermore, she has hosted many programmes on the network, including Fox and Friends, Fox Files, and The Five. Martha currently serves as the anchor and executive editor of The Story with Martha MacCallum.

Since she has a lot of experience with election coverage and debates, her political party affiliations have remained private.
