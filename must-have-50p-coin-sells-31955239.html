'Must have' 50p coin sells for hundreds on eBay but it's 'extremely rare'

Collecting rare coins is a great way to earn extra money.

But you'll never know how much a piece is truly worth until you get it looked at. Now you should check your wallets as one coin sold for a fortune on eBay.

The Kew Gardens 50p is one of the most desirable out there. Often you'll find collectors would spend just about anything to get their hands on one.

It had a starting price of £99 before shooting up to £118 in just one bid. In the end it sold for hundreds times its face value at £170.14 with nine bids.

The seller also slapped a £6.86 Express Delivery fee so it can arrive in good condition. And the bidding war lasted a total of seven days.

In the eBay description, it read: "This Kew Gardens 50p coin from 2009 is a genuine circulated piece and is extremely rare.

"It features a beautiful design that depicts the famous pagoda of the Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew. The denomination of this coin is 50p, and it was issued by Great Britain.

"The coin is from the category of British coins and falls under the subcategory of Decimal Coinage (c.1971-Now). This is a must-have for any coin collector and is bound to be a valuable addition to your collection.

"Don't miss out on the opportunity to own this rare piece of British coinage history. A genuine 2009 Kew Gardens 50p coin in great condition from circulation."

It's always worth checking your wallets to see how much your coins are truly worth. According to the Royal Mint, a Kew Gardens 50p coin could go for £895.

There's only 210,000 of these coins in circulation after its release date back in 2009. It was made to commemorate the 250th anniversary of Kew Gardens.

Recently, we saw a rare Kew Gardens 50p coin sells for hundreds on eBay – after securing 31 bids. Brits are often eager to get their claws on the piece as it sold for £200.

A whopping 31 bids were made for the "rare 100% genuine Kew Gardens 50p coin in stunning circulated condition". A total of 12 bidders were desperate to scoop the coin.
