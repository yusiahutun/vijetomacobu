China Doll Plant Growing Guide

Many houseplants, such as parlor palms, have been grown and cultivated indoors since Victorian times. But China doll plants are relative newcomers to the world of houseplants. Like the money tree, China doll plants first became available as houseplants in the 1980s.

China dolls make a pretty desktop plant when young, with a full, bushy form and glossy foliage. As they grow, China dolls become more tree-like with woody stems. In the wild, they can grow to 90 feet tall! As a houseplant, they will top out at around 4 to 6 feet tall.

Native to southern and eastern Asia, China doll plants (Radermachera sinica) can grow outdoors in USDA Hardiness zones 10 and 11. (Find your zone here.) In their native environment, they have pretty trumpet-shaped flowers, though this plant rarely blooms indoors.

These plants are moderately-fast growers, if given the right growing conditions. However, China dolls are finicky about light and watering.

China Doll Basic Info

  • Common Name: China doll
  • Botanical Name: Radermachera sinica
  • Plant Family: Bignoniaceae
  • Type of Plant: Broadleaf evergreen
  • Native Origin: Southern and eastern Asia
  • Sun Exposure: Full sun to part shade
  • Mature Size: 4 to 6 feet tall as a houseplant
  • Hardiness Zones: 10 to 11
  • Toxic to Pets: No

What Kind of Light Does a China Doll Plant Need?

Like many houseplants, China dolls like bright, indirect light. A south, east, or west-facing window is best for them. The more light they get, the faster they’ll grow.

But if they don’t get enough light, they tend to get leggy and scraggly-looking.

How Do You Care for a China Doll Plant?

China dolls prefer average indoor temperatures between 65 and 75°F.

Basically, if its comfortable for you, it’s comfortable for them. They also prefer humidity a little higher than average, which can be tough to achieve in most homes, especially in the winter.

To boost humidity, place it on a pebble tray filled with water, or group it with other plants. Plants will transpire, or release a little moisture into the air, so they will be mutually beneficial to each other. You can also use a home humidifier.

China dolls like to stay moist, but not sopping wet. Make sure your planter or pot has a drain hole, and water when the top inch or so of soil feels dry. Then dump out any excess water from the saucer. They also benefit from a general-purpose houseplant fertilizer once or twice a year.

While a China doll in the right environment is a charming plant, it’s fair to say it’s fussier and less forgiving than many other types of low-maintenance houseplants. This is no easy-care houseplant such as pothos, snake plant or prayer plant.

The bottom line is that China doll does not like to be over- or under-watered or to be moved too often. if you forget to water it, or if you overdo it, or if you move it to a new place indoors, it likely will sulk and drop leaves.

So, find a spot where it’s happy, and leave it there. If it gets what it wants, it should live for many years.

Should You Prune a China Doll Plant?

If your China doll is looking a little lanky, give it a pruning all around to encourage new, bushy growth.

But if it doesn’t produce a pleasing form with its new growth, it’s totally fine to compost this plant and start over. Fortunately, unlike more expensive plants such as the semi-pricey Pink Princess philodendron, it’s a relatively inexpensive plant, often costing $20 or less.
