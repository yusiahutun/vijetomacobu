Andres Arango: Pastor Resigns after Incorrectly Performing Thousands of Baptisms

  • Father Andres Arango of St. Gregory Catholic Church in Phoenix, Arizona, confirmed he had resigned following a significant error for over 20 years
  • The Catholic Church confirmed that over 1,000 baptisms Arango performed were invalid because of the wrong wording he used during baptisms
  • The Catholic Diocese of Phoenix confirmed on its website that anyone baptised by Arango up until June 17, 2021, was expected to repeat the rite

A Catholic priest has tendered his resignation after realising he had been performing baptisms incorrectly for over 20 years, rendering the rite invalid for thousands of people.

As he administered the ritual, Rev. Andres Arang, based in Arizona, US, would say "We baptise you" instead of "I baptise you" as per the Vatican's instruction.

The revelations were made to parishioners by Father Thomas Olmsted in a letter dated January 14, 2022, as reported by CBS News.

"If you were baptised using the wrong words, that means your baptism is invalid, and you are not baptized. You will need to be baptized," Olmsted wrote in a message to the parishioners.

Olmsted explained that using 'We' was wrong in that, it is not the community that baptises a person, rather, Christ alone, who presides at all of the sacraments.

No bad intentions

The Diocese of Phoenix added that it did not believe Arango had any intentions to harm the faithful or deprive them of the grace of baptism and the sacraments.

Olmsted further pledged to take every step necessary to remedy the situation for everyone who had been affected by the mishap.

"I too am sincerely sorry that this error has resulted in disruption to the sacramental lives of a number of the faithful. ," Olmsted said.

He said that after a careful study by diocesan officials all of the baptisms Arango performed up until June 17, 2021, were presumed invalid but those performed after that date are presumed valid and there will be no need to repeat them.

Arango previously served in parishes in Phoenix, Brazil and San Diego, according to the Catholic News Service. The diocese has created a website for those who believe their baptism was invalid.

Andres Arango apologises

In a statement, Arango asked his parishioners for forgiveness and announced that he had resigned as pastor of the St. Gregory parish in Phoenix, effective February 1.

"It saddens me to learn that I have performed invalid baptisms throughout my ministry as a priest by regularly using an incorrect formula," he said. "I deeply regret my error and how this has affected numerous people in your parish and elsewhere ... I sincerely apologize for any inconvenience my actions have caused and genuinely ask for your prayers, forgiveness, and understanding," Arango added.

Dozens of people attended a farewell celebration for Father Arango in January and some St. Gregory parishioners have launched a petition asking that Arango stay on as pastor.

According to the Diocese of Phoenix, Arango remains in good standing as a priest and has not disqualified himself from his vocation and ministry.

No one, including priests, is allowed to add, remove, or change anything in the liturgy on his own authority according to Olmsted who cited Vatican teachings.
