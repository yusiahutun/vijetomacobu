Kerry Katona's rocky relationship history from boyband star husband to abusive ex

Kerry Katona recently told the Loose Women panel of her time with ex-husband George Kay and the star broke down as she spoke about her new memoir, and third book, Kerry Katona: Whole Again - Love, Life and Me: My Story.

The Atomic Kitten singer was quizzed about her volatile relationship with George Kay, who died in 2019.

When asked why she kept details of their relationship secret, Kerry said: "The fear, the fear from George was so severe. Lily and Molly [her daughters with ex Brian McFadden] would come home from school and I'd say, 'just go upstairs'. I'll never forgive myself for that."

As she became emotional, Linda asked Kerry if she tried to 'shield the kids' from what was going on, to which she replied: "They never saw anything but they heard it a lot of it."

But who did Kerry Katona date before George Kay and why did they split up?

Here's everything you need to know.

Brian McFadden

Former Westlife star Brian McFadden first met Kerry when the boyband and Atomic Kitten toured together in 1999.

Three months after meeting, Brian proposed to the singer and they welcomed daughter Molly in August 2001.

They then had a huge celebrity wedding in 2002, surrounded by their nearest and dearest.

A year after the nuptials, their daughter Lily-Sue was born.

However, the once loved-up pair separated in 2004, shocking the world and their fans.

The pair had a messy divorce but the former Westlife star later confirmed that they had patched up their differences as they both moved on with new partners.

Dave Cunningham

Following her bitter split from Brian, Kerry rekindled with her childhood sweetheart Dave Cunningham.

The pair then got engaged in 2005 but they split soon after a row while on holiday in Spain, when Kerry reportedly discovered decorator Dave had been seeing his ex-girlfriend behind her back.

A source told the People at the time: "Kerry is devastated. After everything she has gone through this year she truly believed Dave could be her saviour.

"Then the shocking revelations about his ex emerged. But because her private life has been made public before, she made a pact not to discuss the split."

Mark Croft

After her split from Dave, Kerry met taxi driver Mark Croft and the pair soon got engaged.

The couple tied the knot on Valentine's Day in 2007 and they share two children together, daughter Heidi and son Maxwell.

However, the marriage wasn't perfect as during their time together, Kerry, who was once worth an estimated £6.8million, was declared bankrupt.

During the relationship, the Atomic Kitten star made her infamous This Morning appearance during which her slurred speech and behaviour caused concern with host Phillip Schofield and with viewers.

Kerry admitted she was addicted to cocaine and that she'd go on 'three-day coke binges' with Mark.

She said: "I hated my life. I'd lie in bed for three days in a row doing cocaine with Mark. We did coke in our en suite and had a lock on the door. The kids knew if the bathroom door was locked they mustn't come in."

The couple then split in 2010, with Mark later admitting that he 'bled her dry'.

George Kay

In 2012, Kerry met ex-rugby player George Kay when he worked as her driver.

A few months later, George proposed to Kerry at the top of the Blackpool Tower.

The singer then fell pregnant with daughter Dylan-Jorge in April 2014 and the couple got married later that year.

However, two months after they tied the knot, their relationship took a turn as George was hospitalised with 'signs of a stroke' in June 2014 and Kerry struggled to cope.

They briefly split after George's behaviour became too difficult for Kerry to deal with, but they got back together and renewed their wedding vows.

The pair's turbulent relationship continued and in June 2017, the couple split for the final time following a string of shocking claims of domestic violence and substance abuse in the marriage - which Kerry previously revealed made her feel 'worthless'.

Kerry then took out a restraining order against her ex-husband.

George died in July 2019, aged 39, from a drug overdose which shocked his ex-wife.

He ate a ball of cocaine in front of horrified hotel staff and suffered a fatal seizure.

During an appearance on Celebrity SAS: Who Dares Wins, filmed in 2020, Kerry broke down in tears while discussing her time with George.

When asked by Ant Middleton about her emotional reaction to one of the challenges, Kerry said: "My mister died last year of a drug overdose. He was really abusive, and it just took me back to feeling really worthless."

In her new memoir, Kerry recalls an incident where her ex-husband attempted to strangle her during a car journey.

She wrote: "I could barely breathe and honestly thought I was done for, when he released his grip, pulled over, opened the passenger side door and dragged me out of the car by my hair.

"I fell, hitting my head on the door. He spat on me, got back in the car and drove off."

James English

Following her split from George, Kerry met Scottish comedian James English and the pair soon hit it off.

The songstress soon plastered her new man over social media but after a few months of dating, the couple called it quits with Kerry announcing the split on Instagram as she spoke about her new single life.

Ryan Mahoney

Kerry then met personal trainer Ryan Mahoney in 2018 and she soon shared a loved-up snap of the pair on social media, claiming that she was 'officially spoken for'.

The couple met on dating app Bumble and seemed very happy together until they split following a make-or-break trip to Thailand a few months after they made their relationship official.

After taking some time away from one another, the pair reignited their flame the following year.

In fact, Kerry announced their reunion in her New! column in 2019, when she confessed that Ryan was a "godsend" to her, after the death of ex-husband George.

Before lockdown was put in place in March 2020, Kerry and Ryan made the big step of moving in together with her children.

And after spending months of lockdown together, Ryan decided to pop the question to Kerry in August 2021.

The couple are still going strong today and are now considering surrogacy after trying to conceive for four years.
