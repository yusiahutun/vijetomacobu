Charges dropped against Citibank VP Blake Fleisig escorted off Delta plane

The man who, along with his girlfriend, allegedly caused a Delta flight to turn around just 20 minutes after takeoff due to a fight with the crew will not face any charges. 

Blake Adam Fleisig, 35, and Anna Christine Koosmann, 36, were both arrested at Minneapolis St Paul International Airport after Delta Flight 2565 was diverted on December 28. 

The chaos allegedly began when Koosmann got up to use the restroom shortly after takeoff and was told to return to her seat by a flight attendant.

Delta said Fleisig and Koosmann 'became aggressive' and created a disruption in the cabin, causing crew members to turn the Los Angeles-bound plane around.

One passenger filmed the couple as they were led off the flight, capturing the moment Fleisig allegedly attacked another passenger when they cheered his arrest. 

Fleisig, the Vice President of Internal Audit Treasury Finance at Citi Bank, was originally charged with disorderly conduct with brawling or fighting.

His attorney, Alex Spiro, told DailyMail.com that Fleisig will not be prosecuted. 

Prosecutor Christopher Renz also confirmed that he does not anticipate any charges, according to the Star Tribune. 

Koosman still faces a disorderly conduct charge and is due in court in February. 

The couple previously denied the charges and called the incident a 'misunderstanding'.   

'We will answer the allegations in court,' they added in a statement provided to Dailymail.com by their attorney.

'You're already going to jail,' the officer who is holding her arms behind her back tells Koosmann.

'Why? For what? For what?' she shoots back as she's led off the plane and some passengers burst into applause.  

Delta said in a statement given to Daily Mail Online that the two passengers had 'refused to follow crew instructions, became aggressive and created a disruption in the cabin'. 

'The passengers were removed by local law enforcement. The flight redeparted without incident,' it continued.   

Delta Flight 2565 arrived in Los Angeles two hours and 34 minutes behind schedule, according to FlightAware.  

Both airport police and the FBI investigated the incident, as is standard when an incident occurs while the plane is in the air.
