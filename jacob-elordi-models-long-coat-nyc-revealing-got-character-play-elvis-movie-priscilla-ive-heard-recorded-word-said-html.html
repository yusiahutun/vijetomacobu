Jacob Elordi models a long coat in NYC after revealing how he got into character to play Elvis for P

Jacob Elordi was channeling Elvis in a long black coat and sunglasses as he left the Today show in New York City on Tuesday morning.

The actor has been promoting his new film Priscilla in which he plays Jailhouse Rock singer Elvis Presley.

The film takes a close look at Elvis' romance with Priscilla Presley when she was just a young teenager until their wedding and when they welcomed daughter Lisa Marie Presley.

During his chat on Today, Jacob - who is best known for his Kissing Booth films - revealed how he got into character.

'I have a deep voice,' he began. 'I think I’ve heard every recorded word that Elvis Presley has ever said and I kind of just have it on my brain,' added the ex of Kaia Gerber (whose current boyfriend Austin Butler has also played Elvis).

Addressing the late singer's mannerisms, he added: 'Yeah, when you watch him move and speak, there’s an energy that kind of comes from sort of deep, deep inside him, so I was just trying to constantly — because I’m a little bit more gawky and awkward than he is - so I was trying to find that charisma in your hips.'

Sofia Coppola directs the film based on Priscilla's 1985 memoir, Elvis And Me.  Cailee Spaeny, 26, plays Priscilla.

Elvis lived from 1935 until 1977.

The biopic will follow the volatile relationship between Elvis and Priscilla, now 78, who met when she was just 14 and he was 24. 

They married in 1967 and had daughter Lisa Marie the following year, before divorcing in 1973.

He also shared that watching the new Priscilla biopic with Priscilla felt 'invasive' – admitting that he felt a little 'stiff' sitting beside her at the Venice Film Festival premiere in September.

He told Hoda Kotb on Tuesday's Today show: 'Watching the film with Priscilla in Venice was entirely surreal and watching her watch her life through her eyes played back to her.'

Hoda, 59, said: 'It must have been wild for you. I mean, you are playing Elvis in this movie.'

Jacob replied: 'Yeah. It felt a little invasive, so I sat there a little stiff.'

During their interview, Jacob also heaped praise on his co-star Cailee, hailing her 'an incredibly talented actress'.

'She was my best friend while we were filming,' he shared. 'Yeah, I really needed that support, like someone that believed in me.' 

He went on to detail how he landed the role, revealing he received an email requesting his attendance at a breakfast with Sofia. 

'I had just got an e-mail saying that Sofia Coppola wanted to have breakfast,' he recalled.

'So, I went to Beverly Hills Hotel and had breakfast with her. She didn't mention it. 

'But then a couple of weeks later, I had the audition slides in my e-mail and they just said "Elvis" on the top of them and that was it.'

Sofia revealed that Priscilla had been 'complimentary' of her work- although initially 'tentative' about the project. 

Speaking with W Magazine, she said: '[Priscilla] was open and complimentary of my work but she was a bit tentative.

'I sent Priscilla the script, and she went through it page by page and gave me specific details, like "I didn't have a teddy bear on my bed".

'But, in the end, I did reinvent some things, like Elvis's bedroom. I wanted him to have a man cave - very dark and mysterious.'

But not everyone has been as enthusiastic, with members of Elvis's estate bashing the project and branding Sofia's writing and directing 'horrible'.

According to TMZ, an official said the whole production 'feels like a college movie', adding: 'The set designs are just horrific, it's not what Graceland looks like'.

However Priscilla has always been very supportive of the project. 

She said of the movie: 'I am very excited to see the interpretation of my book by the masterful Sofia Coppola. She has such an extraordinary perspective and I have always been such an admirer of her work.

'I'm certain this movie will take everyone on an emotional journey.'

The film is set for release on November 3.
