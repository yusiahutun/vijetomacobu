Theory Of Interdependence

published on April 05, 2024
Interdependence theory is a social exchange theory according to which interpersonal interdependence—defined as “ the process by which interacting people influence one another’s experiences”—defines interpersonal relationships. The interdependence concept emphasizes how every living item in a natural system contributes to the balance and well-being of that system. This idea is demonstrated by the mutualistic association between wildflowers and bees. We believe that the most comprehensive theoretical framework for comprehending interdependence and social interaction is the interdependence theory, as developed by Thibaut and Kelley (1959; Kelley & Thibaut, 1978).
