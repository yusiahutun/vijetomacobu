Meaning of I Am Not A Human Being (Demo) by Lil Wayne

In Lil Wayne's song "I Am Not A Human Being (Demo)," the overall theme centers around his larger-than-life persona as a rockstar and his defiance of societal norms. He delves into his rockstar lifestyle and the various aspects of it, showcasing his confidence, wealth, and rebellious nature. The song is a celebration of his status as a superstar and a declaration of his uniqueness. Now, let's dive deeper into some standout lyrics and explore the imaginative themes they help develop.One notable lyric is, "Look up in the sky and see me courting with a cover girl." This line presents the idea of Lil Wayne's larger-than-life image being on display for the world to see. It hints at his ability to captivate and attract attention effortlessly. This theme of idolization and admiration is further developed throughout the song.Another intriguing lyric is, "I make that Nina sing like, 'Shooty-do-dee-wop'." Here, Lil Wayne personifies his gun, referring to it as "Nina." By giving his weapon a voice, he portrays it as a loyal companion, always ready to defend and protect him. This lyric can be seen as a metaphor for the trust Lil Wayne has in himself and his ability to handle any situation.In the line, "I'm colder than a ski shop, I'm holding onto the top," Lil Wayne compares himself to a ski shop, which is typically associated with cold temperatures. This metaphor implies that he remains unfazed by the environment around him, metaphorically representing the criticisms and pressures of fame. He confidently holds onto his position at the top of the music industry, refusing to let anything bring him down.Furthermore, the lyric, "Got a tub full of money, that's a rockstar bath," adds a touch of extravagance to the song. It suggests that Lil Wayne lives a life filled with luxury and indulgence. This theme of decadence ties back to his rockstar persona and the idea of breaking societal boundaries. He is unapologetic in his desire for opulence and uses his wealth as a symbol of his success and status.One of the most thought-provoking lyrics is, "And we don't die, we multiply, and then, we come divide you." Here, Lil Wayne delves into the concept of immortality and the lasting legacy of rockstars. The notion of multiplying and dividing alludes to the continuous influence and impact of rockstars even after they are gone. It suggests that their art and presence transcend time, forever leaving a mark on the world.Overall, the song "I Am Not A Human Being (Demo)" by Lil Wayne encompasses the themes of fame, rebellion, indulgence, and resilience. Through imaginative and creative expressions, Lil Wayne portrays himself as a larger-than-life figure, unapologetically challenging societal norms. The standout lyrics help develop these themes by exploring various aspects of his rockstar lifestyle and highlighting the unconventionalities that make him unique. This song is a manifestation of self-assuredness and a testament to Lil Wayne's journey in the music industry.
